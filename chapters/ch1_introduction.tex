\section{Statement and Significance of the Problems}

In modern educational institutions, students often require quick access to
information and assistance in navigating campus environments. However, traditional
means of support, such as static information boards or human guides, may not always
be available or efficient. With advancements in artificial intelligence and robotics,
interactive robotic assistants have the potential to bridge this gap by providing real-time navigation support, answering queries, and improving overall student
engagement.
The proposed robotic assistant utilizes Simultaneous Localization and Mapping
(SLAM) for autonomous navigation, voice recognition for user interaction, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to generate precise responses based on curriculum data.
By integrating these technologies, the system aims to offer a seamless and intelligent
support mechanism for students within the E12 Building at KMITL.

\section{Goal and Objective of the Study}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Achieve Autonomous Indoor Navigation:} Design and integrate a mobile robotic platform with LiDAR sensors for real-time obstacle detection and SLAM-based navigation within the E12 Building.
\item \textbf{Integrate an LLM for Natural Conversation:} Develop a conversational agent using a Large Language Model to enable natural, context-aware interactions with students.
\item \textbf{Implement RAG for Curriculum-Based Responses:} Utilize Retrieval-Augmented Generation to enhance the LLM's ability to provide precise, curriculum-aligned answers.
\item \textbf{Develop a Robust Data Management System:} Use MySQL and Milvus to store and manage interaction data, curriculum information, and chat history.
\item \textbf{Enable Voice Interaction:} Implement Speech-to-Text (STT) and Text-to-Speech (TTS) pipelines for hands-free interaction.
\item \textbf{Implement a User-Friendly Interface:} Design an accessible interface for students with varying technological proficiency.
\item \textbf{Optimize for Real-Time Performance:} Ensure seamless processing of interactions, navigation, and response generation.
\end{itemize}

\section{Scope of the Study}

This study focuses on the development and evaluation of the robotic assistant within a defined operational environment. The robot is deployed exclusively on the 12th floor of the E12 Building, which serves as the primary testing and demonstration area. All navigation mapping, waypoint configuration, and environmental testing are conducted within this space, and the system is not designed or evaluated for operation beyond this boundary.

The target users of the system are students of the Robotics and Artificial Intelligence (RAI) programme. Interactions are therefore tailored to the needs and contexts relevant to this user group within the building environment. The robot is designed to support core interaction scenarios:  providing navigation guidance to designated locations within the floor, and engaging in general conversation through the LLM-powered dialogue system.

\subsection{Routine}

\begin{enumerate}
\item State 1: Standby Mode \\
The robot positions itself at the starting station and waits for a manual
command or wakeword to transition to State 2.
\item State 2: Walk Around \\
The robot navigates autonomously using SLAM. If the battery is
running low, it returns to State 1. If a person is detected, it transitions to
State 3.
\item State 3: Interact with Speaker \\
The robot engages in conversation using NLP and LLM. Once the
conversation ends, it returns to State 2.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Technical Constraints}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Hardware Limitations
\begin{itemize}
\item Computational Power \\
mini-PC must handle AI and LLM processing efficiently without
excessive heat generation or power consumption.
\item Battery Life \\
Consider energy-efficient motors, sensors, and AI processing to
maximize operational hours.
\item Mechanical Complexity \\
Ensure smooth and natural movement with a robust mobile base,
including actuators and linkages in arms and head.
\item Sensor Accuracy \\
High-resolution cameras and microphones are needed for effective
human interaction, but they must balance cost and power
consumption.
\item Weight Distribution \\
A well-balanced design ensures stability when moving and
interacting with humans.
\end{itemize}
\item Software and AI Limitations
\begin{itemize}
\item Real-Time Processing \\
Delays in speech recognition or posture analysis can affect user
experience. Optimize processing efficiency.
\item LLM Integration \\
Requires fine-tuning and fast response times while maintaining
contextual understanding.
\item Navigation and Mapping \\
Implement SLAM and obstacle detection algorithms for safe and
autonomous movement.
\item Robustness in Unstructured Environments \\
The AI must adapt to varying lighting, noise, and terrain conditions.
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Non-Technical Design Constraints}

\begin{itemize}
\item User Acceptance and Adaptability \\
The robot must be designed for ease of use, ensuring that
individuals of different technological backgrounds can interact with it
effectively.
\item Privacy and Data Security \\
Handling user interactions requires adherence to data
protection policies to maintain confidentiality and security.
\item Cost and Scalability \\
The system should be cost-effective for widespread adoption,
balancing hardware affordability with advanced AI capabilities.
\item Regulatory and Ethical Considerations \\
Compliance with safety regulations and ethical AI deployment
guidelines is essential to prevent bias, ensure transparency, and
promote responsible AI usage.
\end{itemize}
